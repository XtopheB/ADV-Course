Why this matters: colour, iconography and wording are all data too. A palette that maps "women" to pink and "men" to blue, icons that show women only with children and men only with briefcases, or a headline that treats one sex as the norm and the other as the exception — all assert something the underlying indicator never claimed. A neutral, colourblind-safe, legible design lets the numbers make the argument instead of the imagery.
