Key Takeaway

Why this matters: Data visualization is not only about displaying numbers. Colours, icons, wording, ordering and visual metaphors all convey information and can influence interpretation. Mapping women to pink and men to blue, using stereotypical icons, treating one group as the default, or always listing one category first introduces messages that are absent from the data. Even representing a percentage by filling a human silhouette is a design choice: it can make the statistic more relatable, but it may also shift attention from the measure to the person. Good data visualization minimizes these unintended messages, allowing the data—not the design—to tell the story.
